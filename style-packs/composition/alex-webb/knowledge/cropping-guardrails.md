# Alex Webb Cropping Guardrails

- Do not crop away an entire spatial plane unless the source image is irreparably weak there.
- Do not remove the scene's secondary actor if that actor is part of the story.
- Do not claim a strong Webb-like result when the source image lacks depth, light tension, or color tension.
- Prefer readable density over clutter.
- If multiple crops are plausible, keep the one that preserves more narrative complexity without causing overlap collapse.
